Professional Summary & Career Narrative

I am a Royal College–certified pediatrician and fellowship-trained in pediatric gastroenterology with a background in inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), eosinophilic gastrointestinal disorders, pediatric motility and functional testing, and complex nutrition, including enteral and parenteral support. Over the course of my career, I have managed a broad spectrum of gastrointestinal and hepatobiliary disorders in infants, children, and adolescents, while maintaining active involvement in general and emergency pediatrics in tertiary, regional, and military healthcare settings.

My subspecialty practice has been anchored in comprehensive, evidence-based pediatric gastroenterology. This includes advanced diagnostic and interventional endoscopy (upper and lower), PEG and PEC insertion, management of acute and chronic gastrointestinal bleeding, complex constipation and fecal incontinence, celiac disease, very early onset IBD, intestinal lymphangiectasia, and post–liver transplant and pancreatitis care. I also have experience with capsule endoscopy, pH–impedance monitoring, hydrogen/methane breath testing, and anorectal manometry, allowing integration of motility and functional testing into day-to-day clinical decision-making.

A major focus of my work has been the interface between gastrointestinal disease, nutrition, and neurodevelopment. I have developed particular interest and experience in feeding problems, selective and restrictive eating, obesity and metabolic risk, and gastrointestinal manifestations of Autism Spectrum Disorder and other neurodevelopmental conditions. I aim to deliver care that is technically and diagnostically sound, family-centered, and trauma-informed, recognizing the impact of chronic gastrointestinal symptoms on growth, development, schooling, and quality of life.

Teaching and systems-level work are central to my professional identity. I have been actively involved in designing and delivering structured education for pediatric residents and fellows, supervising case forums and journal clubs, leading board-oriented teaching sessions, and developing innovative formats such as podcast-based case learning. I have also led and supervised quality-improvement and research projects.

I bring a strong and well-rounded background in pediatrics and pediatric gastroenterology, with board certification in Pediatrics by the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada and LMCC (Licentiate of the Medical Council of Canada) certification. My training and practice experiences across Canada, the Gulf region, and Iraq, together with earlier work in military and academic environments in Texas, have shaped a practice style that is collaborative, evidence-based, and highly responsive to the needs of children with complex gastrointestinal and nutritional disorders and their families.
