Professional Summary

Royal College–certified pediatrician and fellowship-trained Pediatric Gastroenterologist with extensive experience managing complex gastrointestinal, hepatobiliary, and nutritional disorders in infants, children, and adolescents. Practice anchored in evidence-based care, advanced endoscopy, and the integration of motility and functional testing into routine clinical decision-making. Particular interest in the interface between GI disease, nutrition, and neurodevelopment, including feeding difficulties, restrictive eating, and GI manifestations of Autism Spectrum Disorder and other neurodevelopmental conditions.

Committed medical educator, regularly involved in resident and fellow teaching, board exam– oriented education, case forums, and podcast-based learning initiatives. Experience spans tertiary academic centers, regional hospitals, private practice, and military medicine settings across Canada, Iraq, Saudi Arabia, and the United States.
